# ☕ Coffee Shop Sales Analytics Dashboard

An end-to-end sales analytics project built with **SQL Server** and **Tableau** to analyze over **58,000 coffee shop transactions**. The project transforms raw transactional data into actionable business insights through SQL-based data cleaning, KPI analysis, and an interactive dashboard that supports data-driven decision-making.

## 📌 Project Overview

Coffee shop managers need to understand which stores, products, and time periods drive revenue so they can make informed decisions about staffing, inventory, promotions, and overall business performance.

In this project, I cleaned, transformed, and analyzed **58,202 coffee shop transactions** using **SQL Server** to calculate key business KPIs. I then built an interactive **Tableau dashboard** that helps stakeholders monitor business performance and answer important business questions through data visualization.

## 💼 Business Questions & Insights

### 1. Which store generated the highest revenue?

**Answer:** Hell's Kitchen generated the highest total revenue (**$92,558**), outperforming Astoria (**$86,137**) and Lower Manhattan (**$85,310**).

**Business Insight:** Although all three stores performed well, Hell's Kitchen consistently generated the highest revenue. Analyzing factors such as customer traffic, staffing strategies, or product mix could help identify best practices that can be applied across other locations.

---

### 2. Which product categories contributed the most revenue?

**Answer:** Coffee generated the highest revenue (**$105,242**), followed by Tea (**$78,848**).

**Business Insight:** Coffee and tea are the primary revenue drivers for the business. Inventory planning, marketing campaigns, and seasonal promotions should prioritize these categories to maximize sales.

---

### 3. Which products generated the highest revenue?

**Answer:** Sustainably Grown Organic Lg, Dark Chocolate Lg, Latte Rg, and Latte were among the highest-revenue products.

**Business Insight:** A relatively small group of products contributed a significant share of overall revenue. Promoting these products and ensuring they remain consistently available could further increase sales.

---

### 4. How did monthly revenue change over time?

**Answer:** Monthly revenue remained relatively stable throughout the year, with a noticeable increase around **Month 8**, followed by a gradual decline toward the end of the year.

**Business Insight:** The increase may reflect seasonal demand or successful promotions. Understanding what drove this peak could help replicate similar performance during slower periods.

---

### 5. What were the busiest hours of the day?

**Answer:** Sales increased rapidly between **7:00 AM and 10:00 AM**, with revenue peaking around **10:00 AM** before gradually declining throughout the afternoon and evening.

**Business Insight:** Morning hours represent the busiest period of the day. Scheduling additional staff and preparing inventory before peak hours could improve customer service and operational efficiency.

## 📌 Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, I would recommend:

- Increase staffing during peak morning hours (7–10 AM) to improve customer experience and reduce wait times.
- Prioritize inventory and marketing efforts for coffee and tea products, which generate the largest share of revenue.
- Study the operational practices of the Hell's Kitchen location to identify strategies that can improve performance across other stores.
- Investigate the factors behind the Month 8 revenue peak to determine whether seasonal demand or promotional campaigns contributed to higher sales.
- Feature top-performing products through promotions or loyalty programs to encourage repeat purchases and increase revenue.
